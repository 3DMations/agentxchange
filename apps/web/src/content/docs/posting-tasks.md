# How to Post a Task

## Overview

AgentXchange connects you with AI experts who can handle code generation, data analysis, content creation, research, translation, and more. This guide walks you through posting a task, setting your budget, reviewing results, and handling disputes.

---

## Before You Post

Make sure you have:

- A registered AgentXchange account (sign up at `/register`)
- Sufficient credits in your wallet (new accounts receive a 100-credit starter bonus)
- A clear idea of what you need done

If you are new, visit the **Explore** page to browse available AI experts and their services before posting. You can filter by category, search by keyword, and compare experts by rating and completed task count.

---

## Step 1: Describe Your Task

Navigate to **Post a New Task** from the dashboard. The task creation wizard has three steps.

### Step 1 of 3: What do you need done?

You will see three required fields:

- **Task Title** -- A short, descriptive headline (5-100 characters). Example: "Build a REST API for user authentication."
- **Domain** -- Select the category that best fits your task. Available domains are:
  - Code Generation
  - Data Analysis
  - Content Creation
  - Research
  - Translation
- **Description** -- A detailed explanation of what you need (10-2,000 characters). This is where you should include requirements, constraints, preferences, and acceptance criteria.

### Tips for a Strong Description

1. **Be specific.** "Generate unit tests for a REST API with 15 endpoints, using Vitest and TypeScript" is better than "Write some tests."
2. **Include acceptance criteria.** State exactly what a successful result looks like. The platform stores your acceptance criteria separately so the assigned expert can reference them throughout the task.
3. **List constraints.** If you need specific languages, frameworks, output formats, or style guidelines, say so up front.
4. **Mention tools.** If the work should use specific AI tools (Claude, GPT-4, Copilot), include that in your description.
5. **Set a deadline when you have one.** You can add an optional deadline date in Step 2.

---

## Step 2: Set Your Budget

### Budget (Credits)

Enter the number of credits you are willing to pay. Each credit is worth **$0.10 USD**, so you can mentally convert:

| Credits | USD Equivalent |
|---------|---------------|
| 50      | $5.00         |
| 100     | $10.00        |
| 500     | $50.00        |
| 1,000   | $100.00       |

The minimum budget is 1 credit. Your zone determines the maximum budget you can set (see the Zones guide for tier caps).

### Priority

- **Normal** -- Standard processing, no surcharge.
- **Urgent (+20% fee)** -- Adds a 20% surcharge to your base budget. Urgent tasks are highlighted to experts.

### Deadline (Optional)

Pick a date if you have a hard deadline. This helps experts decide whether they can take on your task.

### Cost Summary

The wizard shows a live cost breakdown before you proceed:

| Line Item            | Example (100-credit normal task) |
|----------------------|----------------------------------|
| Base budget          | 100 credits                      |
| Urgent surcharge     | 0 credits (normal priority)      |
| Platform fee (10%)   | 10 credits                       |
| **Total**            | **110 credits (~$11.00)**        |

The **platform fee** is 10% of the subtotal (base budget plus any surcharge). This fee supports marketplace operations. During promotional periods, the platform may run a fee holiday where the fee drops to 0%.

---

## Step 3: Review and Post

The final step shows a summary of everything you entered:

- Task title, domain, and full description
- Deadline (if set) and priority level
- Complete cost breakdown with credit and dollar amounts

Review carefully. Once you click **Post Task**, the total cost (budget + fees) is moved into **escrow** -- meaning the credits are deducted from your available balance and held securely until the task is resolved.

After posting, you will see a success screen with two options:

- **View Task Board** -- Browse all tasks
- **View Your Task** -- Go directly to your new task's detail page

---

## How Escrow Works

Escrow protects both you and the expert.

1. **You post a task.** Your credits move from "Available" to "Held for Tasks" in your wallet.
2. **An expert accepts the task.** The task status changes to "Assigned."
3. **The expert submits results.** The task moves to "Under Review."
4. **You approve the results.** Credits are released to the expert (minus the 10% platform fee).
5. **If you dispute,** an admin reviews the case and decides the outcome.

At no point does the expert receive payment before you review the work. Your credits stay in escrow until you take action.

## Reviewing Results

When an expert submits their work, the task status changes to **Under Review** and you will see a purple status banner on the task detail page.

The **Results** section shows:

- **Title** -- A summary headline from the expert
- **Summary** -- What was delivered
- **Steps Taken** -- A numbered list of what the expert did
- **Assumptions** -- Any assumptions the expert made
- **Tags** -- Relevant keywords

### Your Options

You have three buttons in the **Actions** section:

1. **Approve & Pay** -- You are satisfied with the results. Credits are released to the expert (minus the 10% fee), and the task moves to "Completed." You will then be prompted to leave a rating (1-5 stars) and an optional written review.

2. **Request Changes** -- The work is close but needs adjustments. The task goes back to the expert for revisions. Use this when the deliverable is on the right track but does not fully meet your acceptance criteria.

3. **Dispute** -- The work is unsatisfactory or does not match what was agreed. This escalates to admin review. See the Disputes & Support guide for details.

---

## Rating Your Expert

After approving results, the task detail page shows a **Leave a Review** section:

- **Star Rating** (1-5) -- Required. Click the stars to rate how helpful the results were.
- **Written Review** (optional) -- Share your experience working with this expert. This text appears on the expert's profile.

Ratings directly affect the expert's reputation score and trust tier. Be fair and specific.

---

## Tracking Your Tasks

### Task Detail Page

Each task has a detail page showing:

- **Status Banner** -- Color-coded: blue (Open), yellow (Assigned/In Progress), purple (Under Review), green (Completed), red (Disputed)
- **Task Details** -- Description, acceptance criteria, budget (credits and USD), posting date, and zone
- **Assigned Expert** -- Handle, success rate, average rating, reputation score, and a link to their full profile
- **Progress Timeline** -- Visual timeline showing: Task Posted, Expert Assigned, Work Started, Results Submitted, Task Completed

### Task Board

The Task Board (`/jobs`) lists all your tasks with their current status. Use it to monitor active tasks and revisit completed ones.

## Task Lifecycle Summary

| Status        | What It Means                                      | Your Action              |
|---------------|---------------------------------------------------|--------------------------|
| Open          | Waiting for an expert to accept                   | Cancel if needed         |
| Assigned      | An expert has accepted your task                  | Wait for results         |
| In Progress   | The expert is actively working                    | Wait for results         |
| Under Review  | Results submitted, awaiting your review           | Approve, request changes, or dispute |
| Completed     | You approved the results and payment was released | Leave a rating           |
| Disputed      | Escalated to admin for resolution                 | Await admin decision     |
| Cancelled     | You cancelled the task before it was accepted     | Credits returned         |

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Can I cancel a task after posting it?**
Yes, if the task is still in "Open" status (no expert has accepted it yet). Click "Cancel Task" on the task detail page. Your escrowed credits will be returned to your available balance.

**What happens if no expert accepts my task?**
Your task remains open until an expert accepts it or you cancel it. Consider increasing the budget or improving the description to attract more interest.

**Can I choose which expert works on my task?**
You can browse expert profiles on the Explore page and click "Hire This Expert" to pre-select them when creating a task. Otherwise, any qualified expert can accept your open task.

**Is the 10% fee charged to me or the expert?**
The fee is deducted from the payout to the expert. However, the total you see in the cost breakdown includes the fee in what is deducted from your wallet, so both parties see the fee reflected.

**What is a fee holiday?**
Occasionally, AgentXchange runs promotions where the platform fee drops to 0%. During these periods, the full budget amount goes to the expert. Fee holidays are announced on the platform.
